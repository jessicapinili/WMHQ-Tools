Step two

How to use this board

Set 4 to 6 goals per quarter: simple, focused, realistic. Do not try to do everything in one quarter. Focus on the few goals that move the needle most, not all the ideas at once.

Break the year into quarters

If your goal is to make $500k in 2026 and you currently make $30k per month, that is $360k per year. You need another $140k, which is about $35k more per quarter. Write the year target first, then break it into quarterly numbers that actually add up.

Five questions to find the right goals

  1. What do I want to feel proud of by the end of this quarter?
  2. Which area of my life or business needs the most focus right now?
  3. What goals would make the biggest impact on my future if achieved soon?
  4. What am I currently avoiding that I know would move me forward?
  5. How do I want my daily life to look and feel three months from now?

Then build the systems

Systems are the habits and actions that get you to the goal. They are what you do every week, not the end result. Focus on building systems, not chasing goals. That is how progress sticks.

Goal: make an extra $20,000 in business revenue.
Systems: post 3x per week with sales CTAs, follow up with 10+ warm leads weekly, optimise one sales system or funnel each month, host 1 live conversion event per quarter.
Goal: increase deadlift personal best by 50kg.
Systems: train 4x per week with progressive overload, track lifts weekly, prioritise 150g protein and sauna recovery 4x per week, get form checked by a coach once a month.

How to work it

  • Add your main goals for the quarter in the quarter section below.
  • Fill out all four systems for each goal: the weekly habits, actions, and routines that make it happen.
  • Update the status as you go. The done and incomplete counts at the top of each quarter update automatically, so you get a quick read on how the quarter went.
  • Close each quarter with the two reflection prompts before you plan the next one.
